Steve Jobs (1955-2011): Visionary Co-founder of Apple

Born in 1955, Steve Jobs co-founded Apple in 1976, contributing to the launch of the Apple I and Macintosh. Despite a temporary departure, he played a pivotal role in founding NeXT and acquiring Pixar. Jobs returned to Apple in 1997, steering the company to unprecedented success with groundbreaking products such as the iMac, iPod, iPhone, and iPad. Known for his visionary leadership, Jobs left an enduring legacy in the tech industry until his passing in 2011.

His journey, marked by innovation and resilience, not only revitalized Apple but also reshaped the entire technology landscape. Jobs' impact extended beyond his role as a co-founder, making him a revered figure in business and design, and his influence continues to resonate in the products and ethos of Apple today.
